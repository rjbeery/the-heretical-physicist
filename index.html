Layman’s Summary

What happens at a black hole’s edge?

Einstein once argued that the “surface” of a black hole could never fully form because light—and therefore all causal contact—would freeze at its boundary. My work revisits that reasoning using modern tools, showing that when you follow the math carefully, no signal or particle ever truly crosses the horizon. The collapse continues, but the surface remains just out of reach, like a film paused on its final frame.

This view doesn’t break relativity—it applies it strictly. In evaporating models, the horizon retreats faster than anything can fall, making crossing impossible even in theory. That picture replaces the idea of an invisible interior with one of an eternally collapsing, “frozen” star.

Publications

The Heretical Physicist: Revisiting Einstein's Objection to the Event Horizon

Reports in Advances of Physical Sciences, Vol. 9 (2025), Open Access.

Revisits Einstein’s causal reasoning about horizon formation using an inelastic rope thought experiment to show that causal contact with infalling matter is never reestablished.

Read on World Scientific

No Passing Zone (Redux): Horizon Chasing in Evaporating Black Holes

Reports in Advances of Physical Sciences, Vol. 9 (2025), Open Access.

Extends the analysis to evaporating black holes using the ingoing Vaidya metric, demonstrating that shrinking horizons always recede faster than infalling observers.

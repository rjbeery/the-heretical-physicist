The Core Argument

Nothing crosses the event horizon—ever

Einstein famously rejected black holes, arguing that gravitational collapse would freeze at the Schwarzschild radius. Modern physics dismisses this as naive, but my work shows Einstein had the right intuition for the wrong reasons.

The mathematical proof: Using relativistic coordinate transformations, I demonstrate that no physical signal or particle ever reaches the horizon in finite coordinate time—from any reference frame that maintains causal contact with the outside universe.

For evaporating black holes, the situation is even more stark: the horizon recedes faster than any infalling matter can approach it. The black hole evaporates completely before anything crosses. This isn't speculation—it's what the Vaidya metric explicitly tells us when we solve for infalling geodesics.

This resurrects the "frozen star" interpretation that dominated before the 1960s. The collapse is real, but completion is a mathematical artifact we can never observe or verify.

The controversy:

These papers directly contradict standard textbook treatments. I show that the usual coordinate-free arguments for horizon crossing contain a subtle error: they assume completion of an infinite process in finite time.

Published Research

The Heretical Physicist: Revisiting Einstein's Objection to the Event Horizon

Reports in Advances of Physical Sciences, Vol. 9 (2025) • Peer-reviewed • Open Access

Uses an inelastic rope thought experiment to prove that causal contact with matter approaching the horizon is permanently severed. The horizon represents a causal boundary that cannot be crossed, not merely a coordinate artifact.

Key result: The proper time to horizon crossing is finite, but the coordinate time is infinite in all frames maintaining external causality.

No Passing Zone (Redux): Horizon Chasing in Evaporating Black Holes

Reports in Advances of Physical Sciences, Vol. 9 (2025) • Peer-reviewed • Open Access

Solves the geodesic equations in the ingoing Vaidya metric to show that evaporating horizons always outrun infalling observers. The black hole disappears completely before anything can cross.

Key result: For any evaporation rate, the horizon recession velocity exceeds the infall velocity at all points outside the horizon.

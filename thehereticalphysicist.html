Core Idea

Black holes as “frozen stars,” not traversable horizons

Einstein argued that event horizons cannot form within any finite external time. My research revisits that objection through explicit causal reasoning: light and matter approaching the Schwarzschild radius become increasingly delayed and redshifted, never completing the crossing. The boundary functions as a zero-way surface — a limit of causal reach rather than a physical gateway.

The first publication presents an accessible thought experiment (the inelastic rope) illustrating how causal communication fails at the horizon. The second formalizes this reasoning in a radiating spacetime, showing that even under dynamic collapse the freeze persists when evaporation is considered.

Together, these analyses suggest that the traditional one-way membrane view of black holes may require revision: what forms instead is an eternally collapsing configuration, observationally indistinguishable from a black hole yet never completing horizon formation.
